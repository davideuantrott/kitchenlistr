❓ Help & FAQ

📖 Recipes

How do I add a recipe? ▼
Click "+ Add Recipe" on the Recipes page (or tap the tomato ➕ button at the bottom-right on mobile). Enter a name, add ingredients one by one or use bulk-paste mode, and save. Each recipe gets a colourful avatar automatically — or you can pick a custom emoji.
Tip: Tick "Title only" for takeaways or restaurant meals so they appear in the meal plan without generating a shopping list.
How do I filter recipes by category? ▼
If you've set up Meal Categories in Settings, a row of filter chips appears at the top of the Recipes page. Tap All to show everything, or tap a category name (e.g. "Dinner") to show only recipes tagged with that category. Recipes can belong to multiple categories.
How do I add a recipe straight to the meal plan? ▼
Tap "+ Add to Plan" on any recipe card, or open a recipe and tap "Add to Plan" in the footer. Choose a date and meal type, then confirm — the recipe is immediately added to your meal plan.
Can I share recipes with others? ▼
Yes! Open a recipe and tap "Share" to generate a link. Anyone with the link can import the recipe into their own account. Use "Share Recipes" on the main Recipes page to share multiple recipes at once.
Can I add an image to a recipe? ▼
Yes! When adding or editing a recipe, paste a public image URL into the Image URL field. The image replaces the automatic colour-and-emoji avatar on the recipe card and in the detail view.

📅 Meal Planning

How do I add a recipe to the meal plan? ▼
Go to the Meal Plan page and tap the ➕ button on any meal slot. Search your recipes, select one, and it's added instantly. Alternatively, use the tomato ➕ FAB button at the bottom-right — it opens the recipe picker for today's next empty meal slot. You can also add recipes directly from the Recipes page using "+ Add to Plan".
How do I navigate between weeks and days? ▼
Use the ‹ › arrows to move back and forward by one week. The day strip (scrollable row of day pills below the navigation) shows all 7 days — tap a day to jump straight to it. The current day is highlighted in tomato.
What's the difference between Week and Month view? ▼
Week view shows each day as a card with all meal slots visible — great for day-to-day planning. Month view shows a full calendar grid where each day cell contains compact colour-coded meal pills (one per meal category). Tap any day in month view to jump to that week.
Can I add a new recipe while planning? ▼
Yes! In the recipe picker, tap "+ New Recipe" to create one on the fly. Enter a name, optionally tick "Title only", and tap "Save & Select" — the recipe is saved and immediately added to the meal slot. Add full ingredients later on the Recipes page.
Can I add custom meal categories? ▼
Yes! Go to Settings → Meal Categories to add, rename, reorder, or remove categories. Categories are shared between Recipes and Meal Planner — the same list is used to tag recipes and to define meal slots on the planner.
What are day notes? ▼
Each day card has a "📝 Add note" link at the bottom. Use it to add reminders like "Guests coming" or "Defrost chicken overnight". Notes appear on the day card in the planner.
Can I print or save the meal plan? ▼
Yes! Click "Print Plan" at the top of the Meal Plan page. Choose Week or Month view, then click Print. In your browser's print dialog, select "Save as PDF" to save a shareable file.
Tip: Landscape orientation usually works best for the week view.

🛒 Shopping List

How does the shopping list work? ▼
Your shopping list is built automatically from the recipes in your meal plan for the selected date range. Tick items off as you shop — ticked items show with a strikethrough. Use the date inputs or quick presets (Today / 3d / 7d / This week / All) to control which dates are included; the ‹ › arrows shift the selected span forward or backward. A progress bar at the top shows how many items you've checked off.
How do I sort the shopping list? ▼
Use the sort chips at the top of the list: A–Z (alphabetical), Aisle (by your shop layout), Date (by meal date), or Recipe (grouped by recipe). Sorting by Aisle is the most useful when you're actually in the shop — it puts items in the order you walk through the aisles.
Can I hide items I've already ticked? ▼
Yes! Tap the "Hide checked" chip in the sort chips row to collapse all ticked items out of view. Tap it again to show them. This is useful when your list is long and you want to focus on what's left to find.
Can I add items that aren't in recipes? ▼
Yes! Use the "Add extra item" input at the top of the shopping list (or tap the tomato ➕ FAB at the bottom-right to jump to it). As you type, suggestions appear from your ingredients and previous extras. Extra items stay until you remove them with the ✕ button.
How do I clear items I've ticked off? ▼
A "Clear checked (N)" floating pill appears at the bottom of the screen whenever you have ticked items. Tap it to mark all ticked items as "not bought" in one go — useful when you're starting a new shop.
What does the Export option do? ▼
Tap "Export ▾" at the top of the Shopping List page to reveal two options:
Copy as Text — copies a formatted list to your clipboard for pasting into a message or note.
Download CSV — downloads a spreadsheet file.
Both exports only include items you still need to buy — ticked items and past-date meals are excluded.
How do I organise items by aisle? ▼
Go to Settings → Shops & Aisles to set up your shop's aisle layout. You can add an emoji to each aisle for quick visual recognition. Then use the Ingredient Dictionary to assign each ingredient to an aisle. Once set up, the Aisle sort option groups your shopping list in the order you walk through the store.

⚙️ Settings

How do I manage my shops and aisles? ▼
Go to Settings → Shops & Aisles. Tap "▼ Aisles" next to a shop to expand or collapse it. Use ▲ ▼ to reorder aisles. Tap the emoji area next to an aisle to assign an emoji — it will appear as a header on your shopping list. You can add multiple shops and switch between them when generating the shopping list.
What is the Ingredient Dictionary? ▼
The Ingredient Dictionary (in Settings) maps each ingredient name to an aisle in your shop. Once assigned, every occurrence of that ingredient in your shopping list is sorted into the correct aisle automatically. Dictionaries are stored per-shop.

👥 Account & Household

What is Household mode? ▼
Household mode lets multiple people share the same recipes, meal plan, and shopping list in real time. All changes sync instantly across everyone's devices. Switch between your personal data and household data using the toggle in the Account tab.
How do I invite someone to my household? ▼
Go to Account → Household Settings and tap "Create Invite Link". Share the link with the person you want to invite. The link expires after 7 days. Once they join, they'll appear in the Members list.
What are the household member roles? ▼
Owner — full control; can delete the household, transfer ownership, and manage all members.
Admin — can invite new members, remove non-owner members, and rename the household.
Member — can view and edit all shared data (recipes, meal plan, shopping list) but cannot manage members.
Is my data saved securely? ▼
Yes. All data is stored in Firebase (Google Cloud) and syncs automatically across your devices. Sign in with the same account to access your data from any browser or device.
Can I install this as an app? ▼
Yes! On iPhone or Android, open the site in your browser, tap the share icon, and select "Add to Home Screen". On desktop Chrome or Edge, look for the install icon in the address bar. The app also works offline for basic browsing when there's no internet connection.
